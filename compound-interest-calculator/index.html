What Is a Compound Interest Calculator?

A compound interest calculator helps you estimate how your investments grow over time when interest is earned on both your initial investment and your accumulated returns. This calculator also includes regular monthly contributions, making it ideal for long-term financial planning.

This calculator estimates investment growth using monthly compounding. The annual interest rate you enter is divided into monthly periods, and interest is applied 12 times per year. Monthly contributions are added consistently and begin earning interest immediately.

How Compound Interest With Monthly Contributions Works

Compound interest means that you earn interest not only on your original investment but also on previously earned interest. When you add monthly contributions, each deposit also begins earning interest, accelerating growth over time.

Monthly vs Annual Compound Growth

Although interest rates are usually quoted annually, investment growth typically occurs on a monthly basis. Monthly compounding results in higher total returns over time compared to annual compounding because interest is calculated more frequently.

This calculator uses monthly compounding to provide a more realistic projection for long-term savings and investment plans.
